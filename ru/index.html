About

Nikita Budnetskiy is an internationally known violinist, currently based in Paris, whose career combines artistic leadership, historically informed performance, and a strong presence on the European concert scene

He is presently Concertmaster of the Royal Opera Orchestra of the Chateau of Versailles and collaborates closely with leading ensembles and conductors in France and across Europe. Among his regular artistic partnerships are Les Musiciens du Prince (Monaco) and Orchestre Matheus, under the direction of Jean-Christophe Spinosi

Nikita has appeared at major international festivals, including the Lucerne Festival, where he collaborated with Gianluca Capuano and Cecilia Bartoli, and the Bayreuth Baroque Opera Festival, contributing to productions at the forefront of today’s baroque performance practice. His work spans baroque, classical, and romantic repertoire, with a particular focus on stylistic depth and expressive clarity

His international touring activity includes performances throughout Europe, as well as solo appearances in Colombia, China, and South Korea. He has collaborated with some of the most distinguished musicians of our time, including Maxim Vengerov, Julian Rachlin, Gidon Kremer, Martha Argerich, and Mischa Maisky

Born in Minsk, Nikita began studying the violin at the age of four. He graduated with highest honors from the Moscow State Tchaikovsky Conservatory, where he studied under Professor Irina Bochkova. At just nineteen, he was appointed soloist of the State Chamber Orchestra of Russia and became a soloist of the Moscow Philharmonic, marking the beginning of his international career

In 2015, he was awarded First Prize at the Johannes Brahms International Competition in Austria
